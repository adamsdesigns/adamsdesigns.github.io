Kinzoo UX/UI design

As Product Designer, I worked with a design director and Kinzoo’s CEO to create a new in-app feature that brings financial literacy to kids within Kinzoo’s existing mobile experience.

Read Case Study

Introduction

Kinzoo is a family messaging app built on trust, safety, and connection.

I was tasked with designing an in-app experience that introduces personal finance and spending, enabling children to use a prepaid card funded and managed by parents or trusted contacts.

Discovery

Interviewing Kinzoo customers

We spoke with customers to learn how their kids earn and spend money, and how to optimize the card experience to be suitable for children.

Money allocation and budgeting

Kids need awareness on how money is spent and for goal setting.

Teaching financial education

Educating their children about the importance of financial discipline.

The need to track their kids money

Overseeing and managing their kids money is difficult.

Age restrictions are important

There were some reservations about online spending.

Define

The problem: Parents want a safe, educational way to teach their kids about money—helping them budget, track spending, and build financial discipline while maintaining age-appropriate controls.

Solution

Buckets

Buckets is a concept that helps kids learn smart money habits by organizing their funds into goals or categories.

It turns spending and saving into a simple, visual experience that encourages thoughtful choices and goal setting. Parents gain a tool to guide financial learning, offering teachable moments within a safe, family-centered environment.

Solution

Turning chores into financial lessons

Everyday chores are turned into teachable moments. Kids complete tasks, earn money, and learn how their choices shape their goals, while parents gain a simple way to reinforce financial responsibility.

Solution

Tools for parents to teach, guide and support

The parent dashboard provides full visibility and control over each child’s spending and progress. Parents can add jobs, create savings buckets, send money, and adjust card settings — all within a safe, easy-to-manage experience that supports their child’s independence.

Outcomes

Positive reactions from investors and customers

Interest from customers and banks

Kinzoo’s main goal was to test the feasibility of this feature with potential investors and customers before developing it. Initial reactions are already showing it has strong potential.

We conducted user tests with Kinzoo customers where they expressed they would love to try this concept with their kids. Kinzoo has connected with a VP at Mastercard and also has interest from RBC ventures for potential investment..
